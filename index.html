Bride recommends

If you are staying for a couple of days before or after the wedding, you should definitely explore Krakow and its surroundings!

  • In the Old City, don’t miss Sukiennice, St. Mary’s Church, Barbakan, Church of St. Francis of Assisi, and Collegium Maius while walking around Planty.
  • Make sure to visit Wawel Castle and its Cathedral, as well as the MOCAK Museum of Contemporary Art.
  • Explore Kazimierz and all its bars, restaurants, cafés and synagogues.
  • Don't forget to visit the Wieliczka Salt Mine.
  • For history enthusiasts, Oskar Schindler’s Factory in Krakow or Auschwitz-Birkenau camp (around 70 km away from Krakow) are must-visit sites.
  • For those interested in traveling further, I would recommend visiting the Tatra Mountains, Zakopane or even going to Warsaw.

PS. I would recommend checking in advance what you want to visit and book tickets since August is quite a busy time in Krakow. Some museums / places might be closed on some of the weekdays.
